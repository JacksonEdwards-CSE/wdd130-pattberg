Idaho

Idaho contains a diverse landscape shaped by volcanic eruptions, ancient ecosystems, and thousands of years of human history. The locations highlighted here represent some of the most unique geological and cultural sites in the state.

Hagerman Fossil Beds

This preservation houses a bed of fossils that were deposited around 3.5 million years ago. This area was an ancient lake that eventually dried up and filled with sediment. The reason we know about these fossils is due to the Bonneville flood, an ancient flood caused by retreating glaciers at the end of the last ice age. The floodwaters carved through the land, exposing some of the fossils. Without the flood, we may still not know about fossils in the Idaho region. The most influential find here are fossils of the Hagerman Horse. This is an extinct species of horse that are native to North America and the predecessor of the American horse, zebra, and donkey.

Craters of the Moon

Craters of the Moon National Monument preserves one of the most unusual volcanic landscapes. This an area of land is barren and almost alien like compared to the rest of Idaho. This land is dark with caves throughout the land. It was created as the great rift was ripping across North America. As it did, this opened the land to let volcanic eruptions reach the surface. Through a series of eruptions, there was a build up of molten lava that eventually solidified. This created the formations we see today that are still semi-active. There are certain active lava flows and fissures.

Celebration Park

Celebration park is an area that shows human history in a simple but meaningful way. This area was considered home to several different Native American tribes for up to 10,000 years. This means that there is evidence of human occupation easily found here. The most obvious are the petroglyphs found carved into rocks. The canvases that the petroglyphs were carved on ‘watermelon rocks’. These rocks were smoothed over by the Bonneville flood, the same flood that opened the land at the Hagerman Fossil Beds. This one small area of Idaho shows the complexity and how two separate events millions of years apart are related to one another.
